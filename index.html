About Me

My name is Xilin and my area of expertise is in sensing, computation and communication. My research aims to understand and interact with complex physical and biological systems, such as human brains, via advancing the technologies of integrated circuits design as well as exploiting new algorithms and emerging material and devices. I am currently a senior engineer at Qualcomm Inc, where I've worked on successful designs including the Industry's first 5G mmWave chipset and the industry's first 14nm wireless transceiver. Previously, I earned my Ph.D. degree under the supervision of Prof. Jan Van der Spiegel (IEEE Life Fellow) at the University of Pennsylvania (Penn). I've received the IEEE Solid-State Circuit Society (SSCS) 2015-16 Predoctoral Achievement Award, the Best Student Paper Award (as the first author) of the 2017 International Symposium on Circuits and Systems (ISCAS), the 1st Place Best Paper Award (as the first author) of the 2015 Biomedical Circuits and Systems Conference (BioCAS), and the Best Paper Award (as the first author) of the 2014 ISCAS in the biomedical Track. I was also the recipient of the Student-Research Preview Award of the 2014 IEEE International Solid-State Circuits Conference (ISSCC).
