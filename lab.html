About Us

The X-lab directed by Prof. Xilin Liu is formed by a diverse group of thinkers/doers who create technologies to make the world a better place. Our research advances integrated circuits (ICs) and systems with integrated artificial intelligence (AI) for advancing human health, 5G telecommunication, and environmental studies. Currently, we conduct interdiciplinary research in (1) biomedical IC design for treating intractable neurological conditions (e.g., Parkinson's disease); (2) ultra-wideband data converters for 5G telecommunications; and (3) multi-functional CMOS sensors designs for environmental monitoring. We are passionate about pushing the boundaries of technologies and advancing human life qualities, espeically for the world’s most vulnerable and underrepresented communities. Learn more about our projects.

Our lab is in the Edward S. Rogers Sr. Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto and the Center for Advancing Neurotechnological Innovation to Application (CRANIA). We are currently collaborating with extraordinary researchers across different deparments at the University of Toronto, the University Health Network (UHN), international universities (e.g., University of Pennsylvania, MIT, Tsinghua University, etc.), and industrial partners. We are strongly committed to promoting equity, diverstiy, and inclusion (EDI) in our research environment. Learn more about working with us.
